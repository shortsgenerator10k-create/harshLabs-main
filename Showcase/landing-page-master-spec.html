01
Audience Layer

Who lands. What they carry.

🤝
Visitor Type
Warm referred lead
They already have pre-trust from whoever sent them. The page's job is not to convince — it's to confirm. They're looking for one thing: "Does this match what I was told?" Your design must not break that expectation in the first 3 seconds.
Critical
📱
Primary Device
Mobile + Desktop equally
This means no half-measures. Every layout, font size, touch target (min 44px), hover state, and scroll interaction must be designed twice — not adapted once. Mobile gets thumb-zone CTAs. Desktop gets mouse micro-interactions. Neither is an afterthought.
Critical
⚡
Attention Window
Under 3 seconds or they leave
The hero section is the only section that matters first. Above the fold must contain: what it is, who it's for, and one visual signal of credibility. No loading animations. No splash screens. No intro text that builds up slowly. Immediate pattern recognition.
Critical
Layer Insight
A warm lead with 3 seconds is not a cold lead with 30 seconds. They enter ready to say yes — your only risk is doing something that makes them say no. Design for confirmation, not persuasion.
02
Psychology Layer

What moves them. Where to place it.

DRIVER 01
Desire for Gain
Show the transformation, not the service. Not "I build landing pages" — "Your idea becomes a working product today." Lead with the after, not the during.
→ Trigger: outcome-first headlines
DRIVER 02
Social Proof
Real faces + recognisable logos. The brain pattern-matches instantly — "people like me trusted this." Photos must feel authentic, never stock. Logos must be ones the visitor has heard of.
→ Trigger: faces above logos always
DRIVER 03
Authority Signal
Numbers, credentials, and specificity. "127 projects delivered" beats "many projects." Specificity signals honesty. Vagueness signals insecurity. Every number must be real and exact.
→ Trigger: specific numbers everywhere
PLACEMENT
Curiosity → Data
Build curiosity first in the hero (withhold the full picture), then land the data section that kills all remaining doubt. The sequence: intrigue → interest → proof → action.
→ Pattern: hook first, stats after scroll
Layer Insight
Gain + Proof + Authority is the exact trust stack Apple uses. The order matters: desire must come before proof. Proof must come before authority. You can't lead with credentials — you lead with what they want, then prove you can deliver it.
03
Page Architecture

Section by section. Pixel by pixel.

Page Blueprint — Scroll Order top → bottom
Section 01 · Above Fold
HERO
  • Outcome-first headline — what they gain, not what you do 3 sec rule
  • One sub-headline that speaks to the referred person specifically
  • Single CTA button — bold, unmissable, no secondary option
  • One authority micro-signal (e.g. "127 projects · 4.9★") tucked under CTA
  • NO navigation links — remove all escape routes above fold
  • Visual that shows the result, not the process
Section 02 · Just Below
SOCIAL PROOF BAR
  • 3–5 real client photos with first name + outcome in one line faces first
  • Brand logos of recognisable clients beneath the faces
  • Scrolling/animated strip — motion signals liveness and volume
  • This section grants subconscious permission to keep scrolling
Section 03
CURIOSITY HOOK
  • A bold statement or question that withholds the full answer
  • Example: "Most freelancers take 2 weeks. Here's why mine take 2 days."
  • This is the scroll trigger — it creates a gap the brain must close
  • No data here yet — pure intrigue, then lead into proof section
Section 04
DATA / AUTHORITY
  • 3–4 specific numbers: projects, speed, rating, countries specificity = trust
  • Authority woven in: "As seen in / Used by / Trusted by"
  • Short 1-line credential beneath each stat
  • Background: slightly different from page to signal a new zone
Section 05
SERVICES / OFFER
  • Named as outcomes, not deliverables — "Your page live in 24hrs" not "Landing Page"
  • Price visible — hiding price destroys trust with warm leads
  • 3 options max — paradox of choice kills conversion
  • Authority woven in: delivery time, revision policy, guarantee
Section 06
MICRO-YES FORM
  • Stage 1: Ask only ONE thing — email or "what do you need?" two-stage
  • CTA on stage 1: "Show me how" or "Start here" — not "Submit"
  • Stage 2: Full form appears after micro-yes — name, details, date
  • Progress bar visible — people finish what they start
  • Trust line beneath form: "No spam · Replied within 2 hours"
Section 07 · Final
SHARE TRIGGER
  • A line so good they screenshot it — the "sticky quote"
  • "The simpler you explain it, the faster you get it. Every time."
  • Share buttons — but framed as "Send this to someone who needs it"
  • This is the viral loop — the page's hidden growth engine
04
Motion & Interaction Layer

How it moves. What it signals.

Hero · 0–300px
Fade up stagger — headline, sub, CTA appear 80ms apart
Feels: controlled, premium, intentional
Signal: "This person knows what they're doing"
Proof Bar · on scroll enter
Horizontal slide-in from left — logos glide into place
Feels: lively, social, real
Signal: "Others are here too — I'm not alone"
Stats · counter animation
Numbers count up from 0 when section enters viewport
Feels: earned, proven, data-backed
Signal: "These numbers are real — they had to be counted"
Services · hover state
Card lifts 4px, border accent slides in from left, price highlights
Feels: responsive, interactive, modern
Signal: "This was built with care"
Form · micro-interaction
Input border glows on focus, button pulses gently at rest
Feels: safe, guided, low-friction
Signal: "This is easy — I can do this"
Motion Rule
Smooth + minimal with intriguing moments. No animation runs longer than 400ms. No element bounces. Every motion has a purpose: reveal, emphasise, or guide. If it doesn't do one of those three — it doesn't exist.

Space Rule
Density with purpose. Every pixel earns its place — but white space is not emptiness, it's confidence. Sections breathe. Content clusters tightly within sections. The contrast between dense and open creates the rhythm that keeps eyes moving down.
